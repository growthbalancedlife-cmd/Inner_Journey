The 9-Month Schedule

Your chronological map through the 10 texts

This curriculum moves from establishing internal control (Stoicism) to exploring the subconscious (Depth Psychology), ultimately culminating in a synthesized personal philosophy.

Months 1-2: Foundations of Resilience

The Stoic Mindset

Establish mental resilience, internal control, and learn to distinguish between what is yours to carry and what belongs to the world.

  • Book 1: Marcus Aurelius ‚Äî Meditations
  • Book 2: Epictetus ‚Äî Discourses and Selected Writings
  • Write a daily reflection identifying what is within your control vs. what is external.
Months 3-4: Depth & The Subconscious

Jungian Psychology & The Shadow

Moving beyond logic, we explore the subconscious. Understanding the shadow self and integrating repressed aspects of your identity.

  • Book 3: Carl Jung ‚Äî Man and His Symbols
  • Book 4: Robert A. Johnson ‚Äî Owning Your Own Shadow
  • Complete the shadow journaling prompt twice a week.
Months 5-6: Systems & Emotional Intelligence

Operationalizing Philosophy

Translate abstract philosophical and psychological concepts into daily, actionable systems. Regulating emotion through structured habits.

  • Book 5: James Clear ‚Äî Atomic Habits
  • Book 6: Daniel Goleman ‚Äî Emotional Intelligence
  • Design your ideal week around your new priorities.
Months 7-8: Meaning & Mastery

Finding the "Why"

Addressing the existential crisis through purpose. Finding meaning in suffering and mastering your chosen domain.

  • Book 7: Viktor Frankl ‚Äî Man's Search for Meaning
  • Book 8: Robert Greene ‚Äî Mastery
  • Book 9: Ryan Holiday ‚Äî The Obstacle is the Way
Month 9: Synthesis

Building The Framework

Review and evolve your learnings. Integrate lessons from all previous texts to construct your final, individualized operating system.

  • Book 10: Ray Dalio ‚Äî Principles: Life and Work
  • Finalize the Framework Builder at the bottom of this guide.

Life Application Exercises

Turning reading into reality

Reading without application is mere entertainment. These exercises ensure the concepts from Marcus Aurelius, Carl Jung, and others take root in your daily life.

üìî The Stoic Evening Reflection (Daily)

Spend 10‚Äì15 minutes each evening with three questions drawn from Meditations: What did I allow to upset me today that was outside my control? How did I act in alignment with my virtues? What will I do differently tomorrow?

üåë Shadow Journaling (Weekly)

Based on Jungian principles, identify one strong emotional reaction or trigger you had this week. Ask yourself: What part of myself am I repressing that this situation is mirroring back to me? Map out recurring dream symbols or emotional triggers.

‚öñÔ∏è The Dichotomy of Control Audit (Monthly)

Draw a line down the center of a page. On the left, list your current major stressors. On the right, pull out the *only* aspects of those stressors you directly control. Cross out everything on the left and focus solely on the right.
